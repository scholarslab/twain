"Then one day he quitted Jefferson for the second time," Mr. Compson told Quentin. "The town should have been accustomed to that by now. Nevertheless, his position had subtly changed, as you will see by the town's reaction to this second return. Because when he came back this time, he was in a sense a public enemy. Perhaps this was because of what he brought back with him this time: the material he brought back this time, as compared to the simple wagon load of wild niggers which he had brought back before. But I don't think so. That is, I think is was a little more involved than the sheer value of his chandeliers and mahoghany and rugs. . . ." (p. 33)
