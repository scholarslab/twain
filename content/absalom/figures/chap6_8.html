". . . And your grandfather never knew if it was Clytie who watched, kept in touch by some means, waited for the day, the moment, to come, the hour when the little boy would be an orphan, and so went herself to fetch him; or it was Judith who did the waiting and the watching and sent Clytie for him that winter, that December of 1871;" (p. 159)

  "And your grandfather did not know either just which of them it was who told him that he was, must be, a negro, who could neither have heard yet nor recognized the term 'nigger,' who even had no word for it in the tongue he knew . . ." (p. 161)
