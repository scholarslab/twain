. . . the two of them, brother and sister, curiously alike as if the difference in sex had merely sharpened the common blood to a terrific, an almost unbearable, similarity, speaking to one another in short brief staccato sentences like slaps, as if they stood breast to breast striking one another in turn, neither making any attempt to guard against the blows.
  Now you cant marry him.
  Why cant I marry him?
  Because he's dead.
  Dead?
  Yes. I killed him. (pp. 139-40)
