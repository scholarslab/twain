It was General Compson who first realised that at this time Sutpen lacked not only the money to spend for drink and conviviality, but the time and inclination as well: that he was at this time completely the slave of his secret and furious impatience, his conviction gained from whatever that recent experience had been--that fever mental or physical--of a need for haste, of time fleeing beneath him, which was to drive him for the next five years--as General Compson computed it, roughly until about nine months before his son was born. (p. 25)
