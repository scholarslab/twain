Letter to James Redpath

Elmira, Apl. 20.

Dear Red —

Warrington's article was delicious. want to go for Timothy one of these days — & shall.


Our tribe are flourishing — the new cub most of all. I was very sorry to hear such sad news of Fall's family, & sincerely hope the calamity will proceed no farther.


I hope to see you all when I come up to the Jubilee. —

a little solo in hopes of getting a chance to sing there.


Could you jam this item into the Advertiser? I hate to see our fine success wholly uncelebrated: Mark Twain's new book, "Roughing It" has sold 43,000 copies in two months & a half. Only 17,000 copies of "The Innocents Abroad" were sold in the first two & a half months.

I order a copy to be sent to you a couple of weeks ago. If it has
