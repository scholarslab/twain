Searching Mark Twain:

Huck Finn, Tom Sawyer, Puddn'head Wilson, and Conn. Yankee

This search tool allows users to search all four works in this database, or to limit a search to any single work. The principal categories are:
  • All
  • Huck Finn
  • Puddn'head Wilson
  • Tom Sawyer
  • Connecticut Yankee
  • Those Extraordinary Twins
  • Innocents Abroad
The results are returned first as a KWIC (keyword in context) one-line display.
The KWIC display leads to a larger context display for any result; from here users can view the search result in the following contexts:
  • Section (usually a chapter)
  • All (the entire work -- this may take a few seconds)
One can also ask for the bibliographic record for the text.
The compound search choice allows one to search for two words or phrases within some proximity to each other. For example, while a simple phrase search for slave child yields no results, a compound search for

slave near child within 120 characters

yields several results. You could further limit this search to a single work, rather than to all of them. When in the "compound search", the works are identified by the followin abbreviations:

Twa2Huc Huckleberry Finn
Twa2Pud Puddn'head Wilson
Twa2Tom Tom Sawyer
TwaYank Connecticut Yankee
TwaInno Innocents Abroad
TwaTwin Those Extraordinary Twins
