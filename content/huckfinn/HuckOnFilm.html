In 1920 William Desmond Taylor made a silent version of Huck Finn. Since then, by my reckoning there have been six major Hollywood productions of The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn or, sometimes, just Huckleberry Finn:
      1930, directed by Norman Taurog, Paramount;
      1939, directed by Richard Thorpe, MGM|Loews;
      1960, directed by Michael Curtiz, MGM;
      1974, directed by Lee Thompson, United Artists;
      1975, directed by Robert Totten, ABC Circle Films
      1993, directed by Stephen Sommers, Disney.

  Taurog made Huck as a companion film to his production of Tom Sawyer; his is the only film on this list that includes Tom in the cast.
  In his essay "Using Audiovisual Material to Teach Huckleberry Finn," Wesley Britten notes several more movies, some animated, some made for television -- see Making Mark Twain Work in the Classroom, ed. James S. Leonard (Durham: Duke University Press, 1999): 230-36.
