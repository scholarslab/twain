Mark Twain & the West, 1861 - 1873


    For the lecture I'm giving I want you to read and study a set of texts and illustrations from the ROUGHING IT section of my website MARK TWAIN IN HIS TIMES: AN ELECTRONIC ARCHIVE. These materials are all organized around the idea of Mapping the West in Words & Pictures. Some of the words are Twain's, but the selections you'll be reading locate his images of the "west" in Roughing It in the context of a representative sampling of what other contemporary Americans said and saw.

    Start by CLICKING HERE -- that will take you to the map homepage. After reading that, go to the map and click on each of these categories: 1. Buffalo, 4. South Pass, 6. Native Americans, and 11. Yosemite. What you'll come to are the texts and illustrations I want you to study; these are listed under OTHER ACCOUNTS and MT'S ACCOUNT. It may look like a lot to read, but the textual selections are mostly short. I know you'll be willing to look at the pictures, but I want you to "read" them too: think just as hard about what image or idea of the "west" they depict.

    I'm looking forward to meeting with you on Thursday. Stephen Railton
