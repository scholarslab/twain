Fourth Volume, Third Annual Revise
New York: Geo. A. Crofutt, Publisher, 1872

LARAMIE CITY

"TRICKS THAT ARE VAIN."--Curious passengers will note that from this city west the railroad laborers--section hands--are all CHINAMEN; they are said to be very reliable; and, as they don't drink whiskey, the saloons along the line are getting almost as scarce as the grasshoppers and the mice. The saloon-men are all "anti-Chinese."
