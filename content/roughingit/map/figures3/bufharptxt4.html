Harper's Weekly Magazine
25 October 1873

[from] BUFFALO BULLS CHARGING HUNTERS.

. . . American sportsmen on the Western prairies say the same thing of hunting the buffaloes, although the freedom of the open prairie makes the sport in the latter case less dangerous than hunting in the dense jungles of the East. A skillful horseman generally manages to elude the charge of the most ferocious bull, and to bring him down with a well-aimed shot, when these formidable guardians of the buffalo herd show fight. Still, there is enough of the element of danger to season the sport and put every hunter on his mettle. Mr. Cary's spirited illustration on this page will give our readers a truthful idea of one of these exciting incidents of buffalo-hunting on the plains.
