Herald Square.--Pudd'nhead Wilson.

Frank Mayo's dramatization of Mark Twain's well-known story, which was originally produced at this house last Spring, returned to the scene of its former success for a two weeks' engagement last evening.

Frank Mayo's work in the title-role is as careful and studied as his work as adapter. His Pudd'nhead realizes perfectly the character so amusingly sketched by Mark Twain--quiet, droll, and convincing.

There have been several changes in the cast since the play was first seen here.

Eleanor Moretti was picturesque and dramatic but affected as Roxy. Frank Campeau was fairly good as Tom Driscoll; Arnold Daly played Chambers acceptably. Adolph Klauber and George Hallton were amusing as the twins. Lucille La Verne played Patsy with intelligence and charm. Frances Grahame was a beautiful Roxy. Others in the cast were competent. The scenery was excellent and the audience was large. Heart of Maryland Oct. 21.

Dramatic Mirror 12 October 1895
