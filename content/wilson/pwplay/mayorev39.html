The New York Times,
Tuesday, April 13, 1897

Harlem Opera House.

"Pudd'nhead Wilson," the late Frank Mayo's stage version of Mark Twain's story, is this week's play at the Harlem Opera House. Theodore Hamilton's acting of the title character is scarcely inferior to that of Mr. Mayo. Edwin Mayo now appears as Blake, the Sheriff. The cast is otherwise satisfactory, and includes a number of actors who have been associated with the play from the very beginning.

All this is equivalent to saying that the performance is most enjoyable, and can be safely recommended to all the playboers of Harlem.
The Dramatic Mirror,
New York, New York
April 17, 1897

HARLEM OPERA HOUSE–Pudd'nhead Wilson, with Theodore Hamilton in the title part, was presented last evening to a large audience.
