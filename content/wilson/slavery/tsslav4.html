Among the diversions that briefly make the village's boys happy after summer begins are a mesmerist, a phrenologist, a circus, the Fourth of July, and the "first of all the negro minstrel shows [that] came to town, and made a sensation." From this list of possible subjects, Williams chose to illustrate the scene of Tom, Joe Harper and other [white] boys, blacked up and putting on their own show.
The earliest minstrel shows of this kind appeared in northern cities in the early 1840s, and soon spread to the rest of the nation. MT loved minstrelsy, and often borrowed from its routines for his comedy. Blackface minstrelsy claimed to represent blacks and their experience in slavery accurately, though the shows almost always represented slaves as "happy darkies" -- except when they were "still longing for the old plantation" after running away to the north.
