As in Pudd'nhead Wilson, one of the twins is challenged to a duel by one of Dawson's Landing's F[irst] F[amily of] V[irginia]'s, but in this text (as you can see by comparing this illustration to the same scene as depicted on page 187 of Pudd'nhead Wilson) the convention of duelling is treated farcically. Neither participant is injured, but all "the seconds and outsiders" are hit by stray bullets. Wilson notes the irony, but silently: if such a duel "had nothing ridiculous about it for these gentlemen, his pointing [that] out would probably not help them to see it."
Chapter 6, Those Extraordinary Twins (1894)
