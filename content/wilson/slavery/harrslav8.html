Most of A. B. Frost's illustrations in Uncle Remus and His Friends are of the animals in Remus' tales -- Brer Rabbit and so on. But in the tale "Why Brother Bull Growls and Grumbles," Brer Bull is a conjured slave. Here we see his transformation taking place in his cabin, in the presence of "de 'oman" and "de boy."
