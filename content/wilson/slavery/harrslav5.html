One of Joe's white friends sends him a message by this "negro on a mule": "I never is see no mule like dis," said the negro, indignantly . . . "I start from town at two o'clocks, an' here 'tis mos' night. I got me a stick an' I hit 'er on one side, an' den she'd shy on t'er side de road, an when I het 'er on dat side, she'd shy on dis side. She been gwine slonchways de whole blessed way."
Harris, On the Plantation (1892)
