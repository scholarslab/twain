This illustration is from the 9th of the book's 14 chapters. By this point in the Civil War even the whites on a Georgia plantation are suffering "poverty and privation." Joe is unhappy with the little he gets to eat, especially "callalou" -- the invention of Jimsy, "an old negro brought over from the West Indies, whose real name was Zimzi, and who always ran away from anybody that scolded him."
Harris, On the Plantation (1892)
