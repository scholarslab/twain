MALVINY SHE LAUGH AN' BITE
DER CORNDER ER HER APUN

This tale was written by Virginia Frazer Boyle, but entirely narrated in "blackvoice" by Jerry, who tells an unidentified auditory about the time, in "dos times afoah de wah," he bought his own wife at a slave auction. In this first illustration we see Jerry (who has been freed by his master, "Marse Jeems," after Jerry saved the cotton crop from burning up) courting Malviny, "ole Miss's maid."
"How Jerry Bought Malviny," Century (October 1890)
