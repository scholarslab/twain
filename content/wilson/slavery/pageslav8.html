This is one of the illustrations that C. S. Reinhart drew in 1893 for a giftbook publication of "Meh Lady." Its narrator is simply referred to as "uncle," and he always refers to its heroine as "Meh Lady." The story "uncle" tells covers a period that begins "in ole Virginia" and ends in the post-Emancipation South. After the War southern ladies have to struggle against new enemies, especially poverty, but just as they maintain their nobility, the relationships between them and their former slaves remain unchanged too. As a "freedman," the tale's narrator finds his deepest fulfillments in serving his "Lady" and telling the story of her heroism. He has no other story he wants to tell.
