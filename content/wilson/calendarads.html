Promoting the Promotional Calendar

  Here are just two versions of the notice that was reprinted around the country while Pudd'nhead Wilson was appearing in The Century:

From the Southwestern Christian Advocate (New Orleans, Louisiana, 18 January 1894):
    The Century Co., 33 East 17th street, New York, have just issued "Pudd'nhead Wilson's Calendar for 1894," containing humorous extracts from Mark Twain's latest story, "Pudd'nhead Wilson," now appearing in The Century. They offer to send a copy of the calendar free to any one who will inclose them a stamp to pay postage.

From the Wisconsin State Register (Portage, Wisconsin, 19 January 1894):
    The Century Co., 33 East 17th street, New York, have just issued "Pudd'nhead Wilson's Calendar for 1894," containing humorous extracts from Mark Twain's latest story, "Pudd'nhead Wilson," now appearing in The Century. They offer to send a copy of the calendar free to any one who will inclose them a stamp to pay postage. Don't fail to get it, or The Century, and read this intensely interesting and instructive story.
    The Century, the queen of magazines, is only $4 a year; or The Century and the State Register may be obtained through the Register, for only $5.
